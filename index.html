About

The most important questions are usually the simplest.

I'm drawn to foundational questions, the ones that sound almost too basic to ask: who is this really for? What do they actually need? Is this worth building? In my experience, those simple questions are the ones that decide everything else.

That's how I ran research for LinkedIn Talent Insights, the fastest product to reach $50M in revenue in LinkedIn's history, and it's why I built Saga.

ORCHESTRA CONDUCTOR → RESEARCHER

Before research, I trained as an orchestral conductor. That's where I learned to take a hundred separate parts and shape them into a single, musical story. Research is similar: turning scattered, messy data into a story a team can rally around.

Research as performance

Distilled insights that inspire action.

I treat research like performance: it should move you. Research that doesn't inspire action isn't music; it's noise. That is the guiding principle behind my four-part research communications framework:

Distilled
Cut through noise to what matters.
Dramatic
Show the consequences of inaction.
Targeted
Specific insights to specific people.
Timely
Deliver insights when decisions are made.
