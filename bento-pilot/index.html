What Bento is

Continuous customer research, without the researcher.

Bento is an app you install on your store. Real shoppers — the ones already on your site — get invited into a 5-minute AI-moderated conversation in exchange for a small gift card. The interviews get scored across three dimensions — how easy it is to buy (Ease), whether your shop feels trustworthy (Trust), and whether the price feels worth it (Value). A pipeline of agents finds patterns across interviews and surfaces the specific fixes that move revenue, ranked by how much you're losing on each.

No surveys to design. No researcher to hire. No reports to interpret. The app delivers what to fix, in plain English, with the actual shopper quotes that prove the problem.

Four steps. One install.

Step 02

Step 03

The pattern
Out-of-stock products show no restock date — shoppers go straight to resellers.

Shoppers who want the product but can't buy it today aren't waiting — they're sourcing it elsewhere. With nothing on the page to anchor them, the sale and the customer relationship both go to a third-party reseller.

I can't buy something that's out of stock, but I still want to buy it — I might need to look at kind of an aftermarket and buy it from a third party. — Shopper, May 4 · returning customer · Discord workaround confirmed
Recommended fix

On each out-of-stock product page, show an estimated restock date if known and add a "notify me" email field directly below the unavailable Add to Cart button. Even an honest "we don't know yet, get notified" beats silent empty stock.

How is this different?

You probably already have tools that try.

The tools below are good at what they do — they just don't tell you why. Bento doesn't replace your reviews app or your helpdesk. It fills the gap none of them can.

Heatmaps & session replay

Show you where shoppers click and how far they scroll. They can't tell you what they were thinking when they bounced, or what they wished was on the page.

Reviews

Frequently asked

Honest answers to the obvious questions.

Will my shoppers actually do this?

Yes — the gift-card incentive is the load-bearing piece. Most shoppers who accept the invitation finish the conversation. Bento targets a small fraction of your traffic per week (you set the volume), so it doesn't blanket every visitor.

The conversations are short, friendly, and one of the few times shoppers feel heard by an online brand. Several pilot merchants have been surprised by how much shoppers want to share.

Won't this annoy my brand experience?

The intercept is small, in your brand colors, and easy to dismiss. It only fires for a small percentage of shoppers per week (default: 5 interviews/week). Once a shopper has been invited, they won't see it again.

If a specific page or campaign is sensitive, you can exclude it from the intercept. You're in control of where Bento talks to shoppers.

How is this different from the survey tool I already have?

Surveys ask shoppers to pick from a list of answers you wrote. The actual reason for a problem rarely fits one of those buckets. Bento has open-ended conversations, so the AI moderator can chase the real "why" with follow-up questions — the same way a UX researcher would.

And surveys mostly catch the people who bought. Bento intercepts mid-shop, so you hear from the ones who were about to leave too.

What about privacy and PII?

Every conversation is run through a redactor before anything is stored. Names, emails, phone numbers, addresses, payment info — all scrubbed automatically. Merchants only ever see the redacted version.

Your data lives in your tenant only. It's never shared, never sold, and never fed into any model that benefits another merchant. Full export and full deletion on request.

How much time does this take from me?

Setup is ~30 minutes the first day. After that, the only thing you have to do is read the insights when they land. Most pilot merchants check in once a week and act on the issues that matter.

The dashboard is built to be scannable in 60 seconds. If a shopper raises something urgent — like a sale being lost to an avoidable issue — you'll get an email alert the same day.

What does the gift card cost me?

The default is a $15 store-credit gift card per completed interview. You set this in your settings — can be lower (e.g. $10) or higher. The codes are auto-generated through your store's gift-card system, so it's existing tech, not a new vendor.

At the default cap of 5 interviews/week, that's ~$300/mo in store credit (which is itself revenue you keep most of). Many merchants find that even one fix per quarter pays for the entire program many times over.

What if I don't get any insights?

Honest answer: it takes about 10–20 interviews before patterns are reliable. If by interview 20 you haven't seen at least one fix worth shipping, that's on us — the pilot is free, and we'll work with you to figure out what to adjust.

Most stores see a clear, actionable pattern in their first 5–10 interviews. Some of them are obvious in retrospect; some of them you'd never have guessed.

What platforms does Bento support?

Shopify is the primary integration — one-click install via the Shopify App Store. WooCommerce, Wix, and BigCommerce are on the roadmap; for the pilot, a generic embed snippet works on any e-commerce site that lets you add a script tag.

If you're on a custom-built store and want to be in the pilot, mention it in your application — we've helped a few non-Shopify merchants get installed.
