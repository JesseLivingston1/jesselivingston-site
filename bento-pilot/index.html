How it works

Four steps. One install.

Step 01
Install in one click
From the Shopify App Store. Bento targets ~5 completed interviews per week — enough for patterns to surface fast without overwhelming you.
Step 02
Bento talks to real shoppers
Customers on your site get invited into a 5-minute conversation in exchange for a small gift card you fund (default $15 of store credit, configurable). The AI moderator asks the right follow-ups to get past surface answers.
Step 03
The expensive issues surface
Recurring frictions cluster into issues, ranked by what each one is costing you. Every interview also adds to a knowledge base of your shoppers — the system gets smarter about your store with every conversation.
Step 04
You get specific fixes
Each issue includes a recommended fix, the verbatim shopper quotes, and an estimate of what you're losing. Ranked. You decide what to ship.

The pattern
Out-of-stock products show no restock date — shoppers go straight to resellers.

Shoppers who want the product but can't buy it today aren't waiting — they're sourcing it elsewhere.

I can't buy something that's out of stock, but I still want to buy it — I might need to look at kind of an aftermarket and buy it from a third party. — Shopper, May 4 · returning customer
Recommended fix

On each out-of-stock product page, show an estimated restock date if known and add a "notify me" email field directly below the unavailable Add to Cart button.

Frequently asked questions.

Will my shoppers actually do this?

Based on intercept patterns with a visible incentive, we anticipate roughly a 10% acceptance rate — meaning ~1 in 10 shoppers who see the invitation will start a conversation. Bento intercepts a small fraction of your traffic per week (you set the volume), so it's never blanketing every visitor.

The conversations are short, friendly, and reward shoppers for their time with a gift card to your store. Real-world acceptance numbers are part of what the pilot validates.

Won't this annoy my customers?

The intercept is small, in your brand colors, and easy to dismiss. To collect ~5 completed interviews per week (the default), Bento shows the intercept to roughly 50 shoppers per week — about 1 in 10 shoppers who see it will accept and start a conversation. Once a shopper has seen the invitation, they won't see it again.

How is this different from the survey tool I already have?

Surveys ask shoppers to pick from answers you wrote. The actual reason for a problem rarely fits one of those buckets. Bento has open-ended conversations — the AI moderator chases the real "why" with follow-up questions, the same way a professional customer researcher would.

What about privacy and PII?

Every interview is run through a redactor before storage. Names, emails, phone numbers, addresses, payment details — all scrubbed automatically. Merchants only ever see the redacted version. Shopper email is never collected — the gift-card code is shown on screen at the end of the conversation.

Full details are in our data privacy policy.

How much time does this take from me?

Setup is ~30 minutes the first day. After that, the only thing you have to do is read the insights when they land. Most of the value comes from a once-a-week check-in.

The dashboard is built to be scannable in 60 seconds. We'll also email you when something urgent surfaces.

What's the financial impact of the gift cards?

The default is a $15 store-credit gift card per completed interview. You set this in your settings — can be lower or higher.

Two things to keep in mind: (1) the codes are store credit, redeemable on your site — most of that comes back as revenue when shoppers use them, and (2) at the default cap of ~5 interviews/week that's about $300/mo of credit issued. Even one shipped fix per quarter typically clears that several times over.

What if I don't get useful insights?

Patterns get reliable somewhere around the second or third interview that surfaces the same theme. If after the pilot you haven't seen a fix worth shipping, we'll work with you to figure out what to adjust — targeting, question sequence, sample size.

What platforms does Bento support?

Shopify is the primary integration — one-click install via the Shopify App Store. WooCommerce, Wix, and BigCommerce are on the roadmap.

If you're on a non-Shopify platform and want to be in the pilot, mention it in your application and we'll talk through whether it makes sense.
